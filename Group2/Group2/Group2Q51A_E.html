Q4: Which of the following statements is/are correct about block mountains ? The block mountains are created when faults or cracks in the Earth's crust force materials or blocks of rocks upward or down. The uplifted blocks are Block Mountains or horsts. The intervening dropped blocks are called graben, which can be small or form rift valley systems. These block mountains break up into chunks or blocks and move either up or down.




Q5: Which of the following statements is/are correct about dome mountains ? Dome mountains are formed when large amounts of molten rock or magma push the earth’s crust from underneath. The magma in this case never reaches the top surface of the earth. The Black hills of South Dakota in the USA and the Adirondack Mountains in New York are examples for Dome Mountains.
